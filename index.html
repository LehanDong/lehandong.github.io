About

I recently completed my M.S. in Business Analytics & Risk Management at Johns Hopkins University (2025), and currently hold part-time research assistantships at the Saïd Business School, University of Oxford and the Johns Hopkins Carey Business School. I was previously a research assistant at the PBC School of Finance, Tsinghua University. I am currently looking for a full-time pre-doctoral or research-assistant position before applying to Ph.D. programs in the next cycle.

My work sits at the intersection of econometrics, structural estimation, and natural language processing, organized around a single question: how do decisions — corporate, regulatory, judicial, and labor-market — get made under uncertainty, and how can we measure the structures that shape them? I use panel and structural methods (fixed effects, IV, DiD, MDCEV, Markov regime-switching) alongside transformer-based text analysis (FinBERT, large-corpus NLP) to recover latent signals from unstructured data: executive rhetoric, regulatory filings, judicial rulings, consumer transactions, and occupational records.

I am particularly interested in gender and labor markets, the fairness properties of algorithmic systems deployed in high-stakes settings, and projects that connect structural demand estimation to operational and policy decisions.

Research

2026

CEO Sentiment and Multinational Strategic Repositioning in China

Human–Algorithm Interaction Lab, Oxford Saïd Business School

Constructed a 572 firm-year panel from 3,300 earnings calls and 10-K/20-F filings (60 firms, 11 years), linking FinBERT-scored CEO sentiment on China to firm strategy classified as exit, maintain, or double-down. Estimated fixed-effects, ordered logit, and IV specifications with reverse-causality, placebo, and first-difference tests to assess whether executive rhetoric causally predicts strategic shifts.

2026

ESG Performance and the Cost of Capital of Chinese Listed Firms

Independent project

Built a firm-year panel of A-share listed firms (2013–2024) to examine how ESG performance affects the weighted average cost of capital. Estimated two-way fixed-effects models with IV and lagged specifications to address endogeneity. Found that stronger ESG performance is associated with lower capital costs, driven primarily by reductions in equity financing costs and varying by firm size and information environment.

2025 – present

Structural Demand Estimation for Grocery Retail Pricing

Johns Hopkins Carey Business School

Estimated an MDCEV (Multiple Discrete-Continuous Extreme Value) model on 36.2M grocery transactions to jointly recover within-trip product selection and quantity decisions — satiation, price sensitivity, and cross-product substitution — via L-BFGS-B in Python. Building a two-stage demand framework that pairs MDCEV with a Poisson/Negative Binomial visit-arrival model, producing direct inputs to pricing optimization and newsvendor inventory problems.

2025

Mortgage Lending Equity in the South Atlantic: A CRA-Linked HMDA Analysis

Independent project

Analyzed 10M+ HMDA records across 593,033 loan applications using ten logistic specifications to identify approval and pricing disparities for minority and low-income borrowers. Uncovered a paradox: CRA-covered lenders showed higher approval rates but charged elevated interest spreads to minority borrowers — illustrating how regulation and semi-automated underwriting interact to shape credit access.

2025

From Judicial Bias to Algorithmic Injustice: Governing Fairness in AI-Augmented Decision Systems

Independent project

Audited 233 domestic-violence self-defense rulings across 32 Chinese provinces using OLS, logit, and negative binomial regressions (p < 0.001) to quantify sentencing disparities and victim-blaming patterns. Developed a governance framework for judicial AI proposing bias-aware optimization, counterfactual data augmentation, and institutional oversight — with implications for fairness testing in high-stakes AI systems.

2024 – 2025

Seigniorage, Tariff Shocks, and Cross-Border Capital Flows

Center for International Finance and Economics, Tsinghua PBC School of Finance

Constructed quarterly seigniorage measures from 1951–2026 and estimated Markov regime-switching models to identify structural breaks across monetary regimes. Analyzed Trump-era tariff shocks across the U.S., U.K., Japan, and EU using DiD and panel FE estimators on sectoral employment and firm productivity. Supported analysis of the Hengqin cross-border capital account liberalization project.

2024

Occupational Gender Segregation in China, 2012–2022 — Undergraduate Thesis

Henan University & Victoria University, Joint B.S. Thesis

Constructed an occupation-by-year panel from Chinese labor-force microdata (2012–2022) and decomposed gender segregation using Duncan and Karmel–MacLachlan indices. Examined how industry composition, educational attainment, and regional development jointly shape the persistence of female under-representation across occupations, with attention to shifts during and after the COVID-19 period.
